Skin Care Ritual

Barrier repair · OCD skin · Dry skin protocol

Morning goal: hydrate, protect the barrier, prep for the day. Keep it light — heavy products in the morning feel uncomfortable and can pill under clothes/headwear.
Step by step
1
Rinse with lukewarm water only
No cleanser in the morning — your skin isn't dirty, just slightly dehydrated from sleep. Water only, max 30 seconds. Pat dry gently with a soft towel, never rub.
barrier protection
2
Aloe vera gel — whole face
Apply a thin layer while skin is still slightly damp. Aloe is your first hydration layer — it preps the skin to absorb everything that comes after. Let it sink in 1 min.
humectant · soothing
3
Neutralgen Hydroboost Aquagel
Lightweight gel layer on top of aloe. This seals in the first hydration layer without heaviness. Perfect under other products. Small amount, press into skin.
deep hydration
4
iUNIK Beta-Glucan Daily Moisture Cream
Beta-glucan is one of the best ingredients for damaged skin barriers — it actively helps repair and rebuild. Use this as your main moisturizer in the morning (lighter than the others).
barrier repair ⭐
5
CeraVe Moisturizing Lotion — hands
After doing your face, apply CeraVe lotion to your hands immediately. Starting the day with moisturized hands makes the OCD washing less damaging throughout the day.
OCD habit — do this every morning
!
⚠️ Missing: SPF — add one ASAP
You don't have an SPF in your current products. Sun damage undoes all barrier repair work and worsens dry skin significantly. Look for: Altruist SPF 50 Fluid (~€4 at pharmacie), or La Roche-Posay Anthelios Invisible Fluid. Apply as the absolute last step every morning.
add to grocery list
Night goal: repair and deeply seal. At night your skin goes into recovery mode — this is when the heavy products work best. Layer from lightest to heaviest, always.
Step by step
1
Gentle cleanse — lukewarm water + minimal cleanser
Use a gentle syndet cleanser if you have one (Dove Sensitive, Bioderma Atoderm). If not, lukewarm water only for now. Max 60 seconds. Never hot water — it destroys the lipid barrier instantly. Pat dry.
never rub, always pat
2
Aloe vera gel — whole face + neck
First hydration layer while skin is still slightly damp from cleanse. Apply generously. The moisture you trap right after cleansing is more effective than anything you add later.
soothing · calming redness
3
Neutralgen Hydroboost Aquagel
Second hydration layer on top of aloe. Let it absorb 1–2 min before next step.
hyaluronic acid boost
4
Beauty of Joseon Dynasty Dream Cream
Rich in ceramides, niacinamide, hyaluronic acid and ginseng — this is your star night repair cream. Niacinamide actively strengthens the skin barrier over time, which is exactly what you need. Apply a generous layer, press don't rub.
ceramides + niacinamide ⭐
5
Dr. Althea 345 Relief Cream
Apply on any areas that are red, irritated or damaged from washing. This is your targeted repair step — focus on cheeks, around nose, chin, any reactive zones. Can also layer on top of Dynasty Cream on very dry areas.
targeted relief
6
Vaseline OR Shea Butter — occlusive seal
The final step — seals everything underneath and prevents overnight moisture loss (called TEWL). Vaseline is lighter and non-comedogenic. Shea butter is richer and more nourishing. Use Vaseline on face, Shea butter on hands and body. A very thin layer is enough — just enough to make skin feel slightly tacky.
slugging · locks everything in
7
Vaseline Intensive Care — hands, thick layer
Apply a thick layer on hands as the very last thing before sleep. If you can tolerate it, wear thin cotton gloves overnight — it transforms damaged hands within a week. This is the single most impactful thing for OCD hand recovery.
OCD recovery — non-negotiable
For OCD overwashing: the goal isn't to stop washing completely — it's to immediately repair the barrier after every wash so damage doesn't accumulate. Make this automatic.
The 20-second wash rule
  • Set a timer on your phone for 20 seconds before you start washing
  • Use lukewarm water only — never hot (hot = barrier destruction)
  • Use a fragrance-free liquid soap if possible (Sanex Zero, Dove Sensitive)
  • When the timer ends — stop. The compulsion wants more. The timer is your anchor.
  • Pat dry immediately — never air dry (evaporation pulls moisture from the skin)
After EVERY wash — the 2-product rule
  • Immediately after drying: CeraVe Moisturizing Lotion — apply to still-slightly-damp hands, rub in fully
  • If hands are very irritated/red: add a thin layer of Vaseline on top to seal
  • Keep your CeraVe lotion next to every sink you use — kitchen, bathroom, work
  • The moment of applying cream is also a moment to pause and breathe — use it as a reset ritual
Weekly repair treatment (Sunday night)
  • After your full PM routine: apply a very thick layer of shea butter to both hands
  • Put on thin cotton gloves (€3 at pharmacie or Amazon)
  • Sleep with the gloves on
  • In the morning: hands will feel noticeably softer and less cracked
  • Do this every Sunday — results visible within 2–3 weeks
Products to add to your list

  • Cotton gloves for overnight treatment (~€3 pharmacie)
  • SPF face fluid for morning routine
